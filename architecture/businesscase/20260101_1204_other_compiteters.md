## 1. The Existing Competition

### The "Big Three" (General Political CRMs)

* **NationBuilder:** The world leader. It combines a website, database, and email system. It is excellent for "Movements," but it is **not** a multi-tenant platform for multiple parties to coexist—it is a single-party tool.
* **NGP VAN / EveryAction:** The powerhouse for the US Democratic Party. It is deeply focused on "Voter Files" and fundraising compliance. Its weakness is that it is often too "American-centric" and complex for global grassroots parties.
* **Qomon:** A modern European competitor focused on "Field Organizing" and mobile apps for canvassing. It is very user-friendly but less focused on internal party *governance* (like elections and leader scoring).

### Specialized Tools

* **CiviCRM:** An open-source favorite for NGOs. It handles membership well but requires a high level of technical skill to manage and isn't built for a "SaaS revenue" model.
* **Ecanvasser:** Strictly focused on "The Ground Game"—mapping voters and door-knocking.

## 2. Where the Competitors are Failing (Your Gap)

Most existing software treats a political party like a **Sales Funnel** (get a voter -> get a donation -> get a vote). They ignore the **Internal Health** of the party.

**The gaps you can fill:**

1. **Strict Geographical Hierarchy:** Most tools use "tags" or "lists." They don't enforce a formal hierarchy where a Ward belongs to a District, which belongs to a Province.
2. **Internal Democracy:** Almost none of these tools have a built-in module for **Internal Party Elections** or **Committee Management**.
3. **The "Platform" Business Model:** NationBuilder is for *one* party. Your vision is a **SaaS for many parties** (a "Political App Store"), which is a massive blue ocean in many developing or multi-party democracies.

## 3. How to Be Truly Unique: Your "Maximum Value"

To stand out, you shouldn't just build a "Political CRM." You should build a **"Democratic Infrastructure."**

### A. The "Leader Scoring" Engine (Your Secret Sauce)

No major competitor does "Leader Scoring" well.

* **The Unique Angle:** Use data from all your contexts (Attendance in Forums + Funds Raised in Finance + Votes won in Elections) to create a **Merit Score**.
* **Why it wins:** It helps parties move away from "nepotism" to "performance-based" leadership. This is a massive selling point for modern, reformist parties.

### B. "Verifiable" Membership (The Digital ID)

* **The Unique Angle:** While others have "Member Lists," you offer **QR-Code Digital Cards** that act as a "Political Passport."
* **Why it wins:** In many regions, "ghost members" are a problem. A digital card that is scanned at meetings creates a "Proof of Presence" that no current competitor offers out-of-the-box.

### C. The "Geography Shared Kernel"

* **The Unique Angle:** You provide the **National Map** as a service.
* **Why it wins:** A new party can sign up and instantly have the entire country's ward/district structure ready. They don't have to build their own database of locations; you provide it.
