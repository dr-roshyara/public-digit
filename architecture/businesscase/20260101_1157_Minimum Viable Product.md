To bring a "Political OS" to market, you must avoid the trap of building all contexts at once. In politics, the most valuable asset is the **validated member list**. If you can give a party a clean, digital, and geographically organized database of their people, you have already won.

Here is your **Minimum Viable Product (MVP)** strategy.

## üéØ The MVP Definition: "The Digital Backbone"

Your MVP should focus on the transition from paper/Excel to a **Centralized Digital Registry**.

### 1. The "Must-Have" Contexts for MVP

You only need **three** core contexts to launch and start generating revenue:

* **Geography Context (Platform):** A standardized map of the country (Provinces ‚Üí Districts ‚Üí Municipalities ‚Üí Wards). Parties cannot organize without knowing *where* their members are.
* **Membership Management (Tenant):** The ability to create, import, and search members, linked to the Geography Context.
* **DigitalCard Context (Tenant):** The "Hook." Once a member is registered, the system generates a digital ID card with a QR code. This provides immediate visible value to the party leadership and the individual member.

## üõ†Ô∏è The Step-by-Step Launch Path

### Step 1: Platform Setup (The Landlord)

Before you onboard a single party, your "Landlord" must be ready to:

1. **Provision a Tenant:** Create a new database for "Party A" automatically.
2. **Seed Geography:** Pre-load the official national geographical data so the party doesn't have to type it in.
3. **Module Registry:** Enable only the "Membership" and "Digital Card" modules.

### Step 2: The Onboarding Flow (The Revenue Gate)

Build a simple **Subscription Context**.

* **MVP Version:** A manual "Approval" system where you verify the political party, they pay a setup fee, and you toggle their account to "Active." You don't need a complex Stripe automation for the first 5 customers.

### Step 3: Data Migration Utility

Political parties usually have messy Excel sheets.

* **Action:** Build a robust **CSV Import Tool** in the Membership Context. If you can make "Importing 10,000 members" easy and error-free (matching them to the right Ward/District), the party will stay with your platform forever.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Why this MVP works:

1. **Low Risk:** You aren't handling votes or money (Finance/Elections) yet, which carry high legal and security risks.
2. **High Stickiness:** Once a party moves its 50,000 members into your system and issues Digital Cards, it is very hard for them to leave.
3. **Clean Data:** By forcing everyone to use your **Geography Context** from day one, your "Leader Scoring" and "Elections" will work perfectly later because the data structure is already clean.
